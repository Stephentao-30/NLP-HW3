Contributions to DD-Based Decomposition
The last summer (2001) I spend some time implementing and comparing
a number of decision-diagram-based decomposition methods. Here is an incomplete
list of interesting techniques:
BDD-based disjoint-support decomposition
Introduced by V. Bertacco and M. Damiani   [1]
Developed by Y. Matsunaga [2]
Verified by T. Sasao and M. Matsuura [3]
BDD cuts and generalized dominators
Introduced by C. Yang and M. Ciesielski   [4]
Implemented in the logic synthesis system  BDS
1.2 .
BDD traversals and cofactor grouping
Presented by T. Stanion and C. Sechen [5]
Boolean division and factorization using BDDs
Presented by T. Stanion and C. Sechen [6]
Algebraic factorization using ZDDs
Introduced by R. Brayton and C. McMullen [7]
Adapted to ZDD by S.Minato [8]
Extended by H. Sawada et al. [9]
Checking decomposability conditions using BDDs
Introduced by D. Bochmann et al [10]
Further developed by S. Yamashita et al [11]
Recently presented by us [12]
The result of my comparative study will be available soon.
Here is a link to download a  Windows executable
of the disjoint-support decomposition software implemented following the
Bertacco-Damiani recursive paradigm. In order to optimize this software
for speed some specialized DD-based operators have been developed, in particular
those listed in [2] and others. The new software seems to be several times
faster than the implementation reported in [2]. To run the program, give
it one command-line argument, a benchmark file in PLA or BLIF format.
