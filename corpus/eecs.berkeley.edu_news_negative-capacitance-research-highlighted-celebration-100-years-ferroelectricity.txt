Negative Capacitance research highlighted in celebration of 100 Years of Ferroelectricity - EECS at Berkeley
Negative Capacitance, a field of research pioneered by EECS Prof. Sayeef Salahuddin, is featured in a Nature Materials article celebrating “A century of ferroelectricity.”  Ferroelectricity is a characteristic of certain materials which have a spontaneous electric polarization that can be reversed by the application of an external electric field. Ferroelectric capacitors are used in sensor applications, like Ultrasound.  To highlight examples of recent advances in the field, the article references a 2008 paper co-written by Salahuddin, which proposed that n egative capacitance could be used to provide voltage amplification and was observed in thin ferroelectric films.
