EECS C191A. Introduction to Quantum Computing I
Catalog Description:  This is the first semester of a multidisciplinary two-semester sequence in Quantum Computing. This semester provides an introduction to fundamental conceptual aspects of quantum mechanics in the language of qubits and quantum gates, and a first introduction to quantum computation.  Topics in part one include basic concepts and results in quantum information, quantum algorithms, and an introduction to quantum error correction.
Units:  4
Also Offered As:  PHYSICS C191A, CHEM C191A
Prerequisites:  Linear Algebra:  Either EECS 16A, Physics 89, Math 54, or equivalent.
Some background in either quantum mechanics (Physics 137A, Chemistry 120A, or equivalent) or discrete mathematics (CS 70, Math 55, or equivalent) is expected.
Formats:  Fall: 3.0 hours of lecture and  1.0 hours of discussion per week
